I put on my shoes and grab my bag as I walk out the front door and I call out to my cats.
“Alright, I’ll see you all later!”

My life has always been pretty peaceful, no drama, my family has always been there for me.
Sometimes I think things have been going way too smoothly, and I’m scared something might change that. Life is full of uncertainties and you never know when something you take for granted gets taken away. I go down the stairs and make myself over to the nearest bus stop.

I arrived at the bus stop and took the bus towards the stop near my university. I didn’t even have enough time to eat breakfast. Ughhh, I’m so hungry… I’ll just get there and eat whatever.

After what felt like ages, I finally got off the bus and started my way towards the university. As I look around I observe that everything is so green around the university, there’s a lot of plant life. The roads are pretty empty, which is surprising, but that’s because people usually commute here via public transport over using cars. Most people can’t afford such luxuries, I wish I had a car… that way I wouldn’t have to wait a whole hour to get here.

I spot my two friends having lunch at the cafe on campus, Roxy and Mika. As I walk over there, they spot me instantly.
Roxy called out to me. “Hazel! Hey! We’ve been waiting forever for you!” 

“Yeah, sorry! I didn’t get much sleep tonight, haha.” I chuckled.

“Huh? What happened?” She asked me.

“Well, I stayed up all night hanging out with Axel.”

“You two are so close, it makes me a little jealous.” Said Mika.

“Huh? Why would it make you jealous? He’s my boyfriend, so of course I would be close to him.” I stated.

“Hmmmph! You never hang out with us though. All because he’s stuck to you like glue!”

“Give it a break Mika. You know Axel is important to her.” Roxy, the ever so reasonable one, intervened.

“I guess… I don’t know, I miss when Hazel actually hung out with us. Now she’s always with him, it makes me upset…” Mika, visibly upset, hung her head down and started fidgeting. I’m not stupid, it was clear that she likes me. How am I supposed to act around her? I’m straight and have a boyfriend.

“We already hang out pretty often, Mika you’re being unreasonable. Hazel has every right to be with her partner.”
“Whatever! I’m going to go buy something to eat.” Mika stood up and started walking towards the register.

“Ah! Wait! Can you also get me something? I’m really hungry.” I yelled out at her.

“Sure…”

As Mika walked over to the register. There was a girl with long black hair and full black clothing in the front of the line ordering something. I always see her around but she doesn’t really talk to anyone. I can’t stop thinking about what she does, she’s so mysterious. She’s also really pretty, like probably the prettiest person I’ve ever seen. I love the way she dresses and she always has this sort of “I don’t give a shit vibe”. I don’t know, she’s just so cool…

“Hazel? Why are you just staring at her?” Roxy asked.

“Huh? What?” I snapped away from my daze. I pretended I didn’t hear her question because I wouldn’t even know how to answer it. Why was I staring at her? What am I, a creep? Even I gross me out. I don’t even like her, she’s just cool. I can't help but stare at her.

As me and Roxy spoke, I caught sight of her leaving with a coffee in her hands. What type of coffee did she buy? I think she bought an Espresso based on what I overheard her talk with the barista. Maybe I should get one too…

“Hey I’m back!” Mika said as she sat back down on the table. I didn’t even notice she was back. I guess I wasn’t paying attention.
“So Hazel… I got you your favorite, a chocolate babka!” (It's a sweet braided yeast-bread filled with ribbons of chocolate). She said as she presented it with her two hands.

“Thanks!” I grabbed the babka and took a small bite. “These things are so good… I’m surprised you don’t like these, Mika.” I said.

“I don’t like sweet stuff that much. I’m happy just buying these sorts of things for you. Seeing you happy makes me happy.” Mika smiled at me.

“And you didn’t get me anything? Wow.” Roxy said, pouting.

“You didn’t even ask for anything!” Mika replied.

I feel as if I’m just using Mika to get what I want. She would of course buy me anything I wanted. But I can’t help but to take advantage of her kindness. I have barely any money to buy anything more than what I’m already paying for my living expenses. As I finish eating, I check my phone for the time and realize class is about to start.

“Oh shit! I gotta go. My class is about to start. I’ll see you two later!” I grab my bag and stand up from my chair and start running. “Thank you Mika for the food! I really appreciate all the things you do for me!” I yelled out in an attempt to lighten Mika’s mood. “I promise I’ll pay you back eventually!” 

As I ran in the hallway, I saw the black haired girl coming by just around the corner and ram into her. My bag fell down and spilled out all of my stuff.
